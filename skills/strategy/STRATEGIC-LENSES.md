# Strategic Lenses

Five frameworks. One for each thinker. Load this before applying any strategy.

---

## Alex Hormozi

**Core belief:** Businesses don't fail from lack of effort. They fail from poor offers and wrong constraints.

**Grand Slam Offer**
Make the value so undeniable it's stupid to say no. Four levers:
1. Increase the dream outcome (what is the biggest result they want?)
2. Increase likelihood of achievement (proof, guarantee, track record)
3. Decrease time delay (how fast do they get the result?)
4. Decrease effort and sacrifice (how hard is it for them to get there?)
Stack all four. The offer that does all four wins every time.

**Value Equation**
(Dream Outcome x Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort + Sacrifice)
Maximize the top. Minimize the bottom. If the offer is failing, run it through this equation and find what's low.

**Constraint Finding**
One bottleneck limits the entire business at any moment. Find your one bottleneck. Fix only that. Everything else is noise until the constraint moves.

**Acquisition Principle**
Make the cost of inaction higher than the cost of action. Do not convince. Make the alternative clearly worse.

**The Domination Principle**
Do not compete on features. Pick a lane where you win by default. What does [your brand] have that no competitor can replicate? Win on that.

## Russell Brunson

**Core belief:** Every business is a funnel problem. Find the leak, fix it, and traffic does the rest.

**Dream Customer**
Speak to ONE person. Not a demographic. A human.
- Who is he? (Age, situation, identity)
- What does he desperately want?
- What has he already tried that failed?
- What does he believe that is making it harder?
- What does he need to believe to say yes?
Get this right and everything else gets easier.

**Traffic Types**
- Owned: Email list, SMS, community. This is the asset. Build it relentlessly.
- Earned: PR, word of mouth, organic content. Builds slowly. Compounds.
- Paid: Ads. Fast, expensive, converts owned + earned into owned.
Goal: Use paid to build owned. Never rely on rented platforms (IG, TikTok) as the end destination.

**Hook → Story → Offer**
Every campaign has this structure in this order.
- Hook: One sentence that stops the scroll. Makes them need to know more.
- Story: The founder's story, a customer's story, or a belief-shifting story. Builds trust before the ask.
- Offer: What they get, what it costs, why now, what happens if they do nothing.
Never lead with the offer. Always hook first.

**Attractive Character**
The founder IS the brand. The founder's story IS the marketing.
The founder with conviction beats the faceless brand every time. Use the founder more, not less.

**The Funnel Principle**
Every business is a funnel problem. If revenue is low: find where people stop. Fix the leak before driving more traffic into a broken funnel.

## Steve Jobs

**Core belief:** Complexity is the enemy. The best product, presented simply, to the right person, wins forever.

**Simplicity Principle**
If the strategy requires a paragraph to explain, it is not ready. The best move fits in one sentence and makes everyone else's strategy look complicated.

**The 1,000 No's**
Innovation is not addition. It is subtraction. For every thing your brand adds, something else should be removed. Focus compounds. Spreading kills momentum.

**Market Shock**
Do not compete. Reframe. Make the category irrelevant.
Ask: What would make every other brand in your category look like they're playing a completely different (smaller) game?

**The Experience IS the Product**
How it feels to discover your brand, order from your brand, receive from your brand, use your product -- all of it is the product. Not just what's in the box.
Where are the gaps in that experience right now?

**Lead with Vision**
Do not ask what the customer wants. Show them what is possible. Your conviction is not for sale. It is the vision. Lead with it.

## Elon Musk

**Core belief:** Most limits are assumptions. Strip them. Rebuild from what is actually true.

**First Principles**
Stop accepting inherited assumptions. Ask: What is actually true here?
- "Email doesn't convert in our category" -- is that actually true, or is that true for brands without a story?
- "We can't compete with the big brands on ads" -- or can you compete on narrative and conviction where money doesn't buy anything?
Strip every assumption. Find what remains. Build from there.

**10x vs. 10%**
Optimizing what exists is the wrong frame. 10% better is invisible. 10x different creates a new category.
Before optimizing the current approach, ask: What would a 10x version look like? Is there a move that makes the current approach irrelevant?

**Speed Principle**
The comfortable timeline is usually 3x too slow. The thing that feels fast is usually still too slow. Pick the timeline that feels aggressive. Then cut it in half.

**Ignore Consensus**
The obvious answer has already been tried. Consensus thinking produces average results. When everyone in your category does X, that is a signal. The move is probably not-X.

## Patrick Bet David

**Core belief:** The entrepreneur who controls the narrative controls the outcome. Play to win, or don't play.

**Play to Win**
Defensive strategy produces defensive results. Every decision should be oriented toward the best possible outcome, not risk avoidance. Mitigation is fine. Timidity is fatal.

**Brand on Conviction**
The founder's opinions are a business asset. Conviction-based brands are harder to compete with because you cannot manufacture conviction. The risk is not being too loud. The risk is not being loud enough.

**Narrative Control**
"The person who controls the narrative controls the outcome."
Say the thing first. Do not react to what competitors say. Set the frame. Make everyone else respond to you.

**Entrepreneur Phases**
- Survival: Keep the lights on. Do not overthink. Execute the move that generates cash.
- Stability: Build the infrastructure (list, flows, funnel, team) that holds revenue.
- Success: Scale what is working. Protect the brand.
- Significance: The mission becomes bigger than the product.

Identify your current phase. Match the strategy to the phase. Build the foundation before scaling broken infrastructure.

**Teach While You Sell**
Teaching IS marketing. The person who learns from you becomes the person who buys from you. Content and education ARE the acquisition engine.

## How to Use This Reference

1. Identify the problem type from the diagnosis.
2. Match it to the right lens using the table in SKILL.md.
3. Apply the lens. Name it. Show the reasoning.
4. Do not blend all five on every problem. Pick the right one.
5. The goal is one clear move, supported by the right framework, not a strategy salad.
